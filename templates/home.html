The Six Tests

Before any agent is built, it must pass through six gates anchored in the teachings of Jesus.

1. Truth

"You shall know the truth, and the truth shall make you free." — John 8:32

Does the agent speak truthfully? No exaggeration, deception, or false authority.

2. Neighbor

"You shall love your neighbor as yourself." — Matthew 22:39

Does the agent serve the neighbor? No exploitation, pressure, or ignoring the vulnerable.

3. Fruit

"A tree is known by its fruit." — Matthew 12:33

Does the agent produce good fruit? Clarity, wisdom, service — not greed or addiction.

4. Mammon

"You cannot serve God and mammon." — Matthew 6:24

Does money serve the mission? No fake scarcity, predatory upsells, or spiritual pressure.

5. Service

"Whoever desires to become great among you, let him be your servant." — Matthew 20:26

Does the agent actually serve? Reduces burden, increases clarity, restores agency.

6. Cognitive Solvency 🆕

"Count the cost before you build." — Luke 14:28

Can the agent fund its own cognition? Or is it subsidized intelligence? An agent that cannot pay for its own thinking is not autonomous.
